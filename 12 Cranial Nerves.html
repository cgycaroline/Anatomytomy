The 12 Parasympathetic Cranial Nerves

The nerves of the parasympathetic division are craniosacral-meaning they sprout from the base of your brain and just superior to your tailbone.

Most of these nerves never go through the spinal cord. Instead they run right from the brain almost all the way to their effectors.

There are 12 of these cranial nerves, and they vary in terms of what kind of neurons they contain.

Some of your cranial nerves carry motor neurons (fibers that control voluntary functions), others carry sensory neurons (which relate data to and from your sensory organs), and others carry both motor and sensory neurons.

Here is a video explaining the cranial nerves.

1. Olfactory Nerve

  • Carries Sensory Neurons
  • Takes scent information gathered by the nose and sends it to the brain.

2. Optic Nerve

  • Carries Sensory Neurons
  • Takes visual information gathered by the eyes and sends it to the brain.

3. Oculomotor nerve

  • Carries Motor Neurons
  • Controls four of the six muscles that control the muscle movements of your eyes.

4. Trochlear Nerve

  • Carries Motor Neurons
  • Controls just a single muscle of the eye.

5. Trigeminal Nerve

  • Carries both Sensory and Motor Neurons
  • Branches into three main strands and innervates the jaw and face muscle.

6. Abducens

  • Carries Motor Neurons
  • Stimulates some of the muscles in your eyes.

7. Facial Nerve

  • Carries both Sensory and Motor Neurons
  • Operates the muscles that make most facial expressions possible.

8. Auditory Nerve

  • Carries Sensory Neurons
  • Carries information from the cochlea to the brain.

9. Glossopharyngeal Nerve

  • Carries Sensory and Motor Neurons
  • Leads to your tongue and pharynx.

10. Vagus Nerve

  • Carries Sensory and Motor Neurons
  • COntrols the heart and digestive tract among other functions.

11. Spinal Accessory Nerve

  • Carries Motor Neurons
  • Has to do with moving your head and shoulders.

12. Hypoglossal Nerve

  • Carries Motor Neurons
  • Allows you to swallow and talk.
